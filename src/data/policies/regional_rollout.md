# Regional Rollout Playbook — Europe

## Classification: Internal — HR Operations

### 1. Rollout Philosophy

> **"Localize the frame, don't translate the slide deck."**

Each European market should receive the same competency framework, but the delivery, language, examples, and pace must be adapted to local context. A one-size-fits-all approach will fail.

### 2. Phased Rollout Plan

#### Wave 1: Anchor Markets (Months 1-3)
- **Countries**: Italy, France, UK
- **Why first**: Highest HR readiness, largest headcount, most brands present
- **Approach**: Full rollout with local customization workshops
- **Success criteria**: >85% manager awareness, >70% self-assessment completion

#### Wave 2: Expansion Markets (Months 4-6)
- **Countries**: Germany, Switzerland, Spain & Portugal
- **Why second**: Medium readiness, requires works council/regulatory navigation
- **Approach**: Adapted rollout with extra change management support
- **Success criteria**: >80% manager awareness, regulatory approval obtained

#### Wave 3: Emerging Markets (Months 7-9)
- **Countries**: Nordics, Eastern Europe
- **Why third**: Need pre-work (trainer certification, material translation)
- **Approach**: Simplified initial rollout, expanding over following quarter
- **Success criteria**: >75% manager awareness, trainer certification complete

### 3. Train-the-Trainer Model

#### Trainer Selection Criteria
1. Bilingual (English + local language)
2. Brand knowledge (2+ brands)
3. Coaching certification (ICF ACC or equivalent)
4. 2+ years facilitating leadership workshops
5. Cultural sensitivity training

#### Certification Program
- **Day 1**: Framework deep-dive + facilitation techniques
- **Day 2**: Practice sessions with peer feedback + role-play scenarios
- **Day 3**: Shadow an experienced facilitator in a live workshop
- **Assessment**: Observed facilitation with rubric evaluation

### 4. Workshop Design (Per Market)

| Element | Duration | Content |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Context Setting | 30 min | Why this framework? Group DNA story + local relevance |
| Framework Walk-through | 45 min | 4 themes, 3 levels, with brand-specific examples |
| Self-Assessment | 20 min | Participants assess themselves on a simplified tool |
| Group Discussion | 30 min | Calibration exercise — "What does Vision look like here?" |
| Action Planning | 15 min | Each participant identifies 1 development priority |
| Q&A | 10 min | Address concerns, collect feedback |

### 5. Change Management — Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| "HQ imposition" perception | High | High | Co-create local examples with brand managers |
| Language barriers | Medium | Medium | Translate all materials; bilingual trainers |
| Assessment fatigue | Medium | Medium | Keep total assessment time < 2 hours |
| Data privacy concerns | High (France/Germany) | High | GDPR-compliant platform; works council approval |
| Budget overrun | Medium | Medium | Phased approach; prioritize Wave 1 |
| Trainer shortfall | High | High | Accelerated certification; partner with external coaches for Wave 1 |

### 6. Measurement Framework

#### Leading Indicators (Monthly)
- Workshop participation rate
- Trainer certification completion
- Manager awareness survey
- Self-assessment completion rate
- Feedback survey NPS

#### Lagging Indicators (Quarterly/Annual)
- Cross-brand mobility rate
- Internal promotion rate
- High-potential retention rate
- 360° feedback response rate
- Leadership pipeline strength score
