# Gucci Group DNA — Internal Policy Document

## Classification: Internal — Confidential

### 1. What is Group DNA?

Group DNA is the shared identity that defines what it means to be a leader in the Gucci Group, regardless of which brand you belong to. It is NOT a prescriptive set of rules — it is a **shared language** that enables leaders across our 9 brands to collaborate, develop, and move across the organization.

### 2. The Four Pillars

| Pillar | Essence | Why It Matters |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Vision** | Seeing beyond the present | Luxury is about anticipating desire, not following demand |
| **Entrepreneurship** | Owner mindset | Each brand leader must act as if the brand is their own business |
| **Passion** | Emotional connection to craft | Quality and heritage are non-negotiable differentiators |
| **Trust** | Integrity + empowerment | High-autonomy organizations only work when trust is the foundation |

### 3. The Operating Principle

> **"Support, not impose."**

Group HR exists to provide platforms, frameworks, and development opportunities. We do NOT dictate how brands should interpret or apply these tools. Each brand's Creative Director, CEO, and leadership team have full autonomy to adapt the framework to their brand's unique DNA.

### 4. Brand Autonomy vs. Group Alignment

This is the central tension, and it is by design. We WANT some tension between Group identity and brand identity because:
- Too much alignment = homogenization → brands lose distinctive appeal
- Too little alignment = fragmentation → no talent mobility, duplicated effort

The sweet spot: **shared language, brand-specific expression.**

### 5. Talent Mobility Vision

Our target is to increase cross-brand mobility from 2.3% to 8% within 3 years. This requires:
- A common competency language that makes leaders assessable across brands
- Career paths that span brand boundaries
- An onboarding process that helps transferred leaders absorb new brand DNA quickly
